Coaching & Tutoring

Whether it's writing novels, short stories, academic articles, personal statements, or dissertations, the process is much the same: writing takes time. It takes multiple drafts. It takes patience, working through bad ideas in order to stumble upon good ones. And, if the project is long enough, it takes unbelievable fortitude and self-confidence. As an actively publishing writer, I am reminded of these challenges daily. But I'm also keenly aware of the rewarding feeling of completing a project, and putting the final polish on a piece. Of sharing my words and ideas with the world. This is why, for almost a decade, I have committed myself to being both a guide and a working professional in the field of writing.

In my capacity as a coach, I've worked with a broad spectrum of students, aged middle school through college, in various aspects of the writing process. In all cases, being a coach has meant both assessing the root of a person's particular problem or fear, and then encouraging and guiding them to succeed in their own goals. Open communication has always been a key component in my pedagogy, particularly when communicating via webcam, where technology mediates my relationship with a student or client.

Editing

As an editor, I have had the opportunity to truly help shape and develop student and client writing so that they are able to communicate more effectively, and to get to the essence of their meaning. Part of helping writers achieve this is to guide them in adhering to academic or professional conventions, both in their grammar, punctuation, and vocabulary, but also in their adherence to particular formatting standards. Over the years, I've fostered the balance between hands-on editing and letting a student's individual voice emerge. I particularly enjoy helping writers to hone their voice, find their tone, and attain a professional standard.
