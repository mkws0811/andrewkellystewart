Whether it's writing novels, short stories, academic articles, personal statements, or dissertations, the process is much the same: writing takes time. It takes multiple drafts. It takes patience, working through bad ideas in order to stumble upon good ones. And, if the project is long enough, it takes unbelievable fortitude and self-confidence.

As an actively publishing writer, I am reminded of these challenges daily. But I'm also keenly aware of the rewarding feeling of completing a project, and putting the final polish on a piece. Of sharing my words and ideas with the world. This is why, for almost a decade, I have committed myself to being both a guide and a working professional in the field of writing.

Coaching & Tutoring

In my capacity as a coach, I've worked with a broad spectrum of students, aged middle school through college, in various aspects of the writing process. With Colton, a 6th grader, the task at hand was to guide him through the process of writing his very own children's book over the course of two years, in which time I coached him through instances of writers' block, self-doubt, and also instilled in him the necessity of revision. With his sister, Grace, it was a challenging AP English class needed to be surmounted, which first required understanding the root of her troublesâa lack of confidence in her reading skills. My charge with Thuan, a first generation High School junior, was to help her overcome the challenges of being a second-language learner and writer in her college admissions essays and, ultimately, engendering in her a feeling of authority to use her own words, to tell her own story.

In all cases, being a coach has meant both assessing the root of a person's particular problem or fear, and then encouraging and guiding them to succeed in their own goals. Open communication has always been a key component in my pedagogy, particularly when communicating via webcam, where technology mediates my relationship with a student or client.

Editing

As an editor, I have had the opportunity to truly help shape and develop student and client writing so that they are able to communicate more effectively, and to get to the essence of their meaning. Part of helping writers achieve this is to guide them in adhering to academic or professional conventions, both in their grammar, punctuation, and vocabulary, but also in their adherence to particular formatting standards. Over the years, I've fostered the balance between hands-on editing and letting a student's individual voice emerge. I particularly enjoy helping writers to hone their voice, find their tone, and attain a professional standard.
